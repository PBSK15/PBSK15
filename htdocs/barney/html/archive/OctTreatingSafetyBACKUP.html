FROM BUGS TO BIRTHDAYS BARNEY & FRIENDS LAUNCHES NEW SEASON OF ADVENTURES ON PBS

New adventures, learning and Dino-MiteTM fun are what children and parents can expect from the sixth season of Barney & Friends premiering November 1st on PBS. The first 10 of 20 new episodes (# 601-610) will air in November and the second half (# 611-620) in April 2000.

In the fall episodes, Barney and the children, along with dino-pals BJ and Baby Bop, investigate the five senses, learn about snow, create and appreciate all forms of art, discover the different animals at the zoo, marvel at insects, and enjoy healthy foods.

The Barney & Friends series continues to be commended for its educational and informational content. In a recent study, the Annenberg Public Policy Center of the University of Pennsylvania gave Barney & Friends high educational and instructional marks (Annenberg Public Policy Center, 1999).

As a mainstay of the PBS Ready to Learn initiative the Barney & Friends show reinforces the development of cognitive, social, emotional and physical development skills for preschool children. Researchers at Yale University noted that "…Barney & Friends continues to be a television series that adds much to our nation's goal to 'ready' children for school" (Singer & Singer, 1999).

The new Barney & Friends season explores themes that are important early-learning concepts for preschoolers. Themes from the new episodes airing this fall are:

  • Imagination (Episode 601)
  • Bugs (Episode 602)
  • Grandparents (Episode 603)
  • Healthy Foods (Episode 604)
  • Snow (Episode 605)
  • Art (Episode 606)
  • Five Senses (Episode 607)
  • Counting/Numbers (Episode 608)
  • The Zoo (Episode 609)
  • Birthdays (Episode 610)
Children can see Barney & Friends on six continents and in more than 100 countries. In the United States the show remains one of the top rated programs for children younger than six. Barney & Friends has been translated and dubbed into many languages including Spanish, German, Italian, Hebrew, Portuguese, Greek, and Mandarin Chinese.

Created in 1988 by Sheryl Leach, Barney was brought to life with the creative assistance of Kathy Parker and Dennis DeShazer. Barney & Friends debuted on Public Television in April 1992.

Barney & Friends is a co-production of The Lyons Group and Connecticut Public Television. The Lyons Group is an entertainment unit of Lyrick Studios, a Dallas-based family entertainment company. Dennis DeShazer is executive producer of the series; Larry Rifkin is executive-in-charge at Connecticut Public Television.
