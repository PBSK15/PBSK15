Keeping Your Child Safe In The Sun

 It is estimated that 80 percent of lifetime sun exposure occurs before the age of 18. And just one bad blistering sunburn during childhood can double the risk of skin cancer later in life. But sun damage can be reduced. Research indicates that regular use of a sunblock with an SPF (sun protection factor) of 15 or higher during the first 18 years of life can lower the risk of certain skin cancers by 78 percent.

Some sun safety tips are:

  • Watch the clock. Try to limit the amount of time you're in direct sun during 10 a.m. and 4 p.m. Think about the shade!
  • Use a sunblock with an SPF of at least 15. If you have fair skin, light-colored eyes and hair, freckles, or spend a lot of time outside, use an SPF 30 or higher. Apply sunblock 15 minutes before you go out. Reapply after prolonged swimming, vigorous activity, sweating or toweling off. Remember your ears, nose, neck and hands. Always cover these areas with sunblock.
  • "Waterproof" your skin. If you're spending a day at the beach or at the pool, cover up with waterproof sunblock. After swimming, toweling off, sweating, and/or vigorous activity, be sure to reapply sunblock.
  • Make a statement with shades, hat and a wild T-shirt. A cool pair of UVA/UVB- blocking sunglasses will protect your eyes like nothing else. As for your hat: if you get really hot, dunk it in water and then pull it on. Of course, a long-sleeve T-shirt is a must for summer fun.
  • Block the burn year round. It's possible to burn all year (that includes cloudy and snowy days). So whether you're walking to school or outside playing don't forget to block the sun, not the fun year round.
  • Share your sunsmarts! Remind your friends and family about these sun safe tips.

To educate children about the importance of sun safety, the American Academy of Dermatology and the Coppertone brand have partnered to initiate a multi-year national sun safety educational campaign. Called Block the Sun, Not the Fun!, the campaign is designed to make kids, the most vulnerable segment of our society, active participants in lifelong practices to protect themselves from the sun's harmful rays.

The Block the Sun, Not the Fun! campaign includes Teaching Guides to be used in the classroom as well as a Family Sun Guide to be used at home.
