Offline Activities


December 1999
Parent Article


When choosing gifts for your little ones this holiday season, keep in mind their age and ability levels. Here are some tips to remember when picking out that perfect holiday present.

Birth to one-year-olds explore with their hands, mouths and eyes to enjoy toys they can touch or squeeze:

  • crib gyms
  • floor activity centers
  • soft dolls or stuffed animals
  • squeaky toys
  • activity quilts

One- to three-year-oldsclimb, jump, walk, throw and play rough and tumble games:

  • soft blocks
  • large balls
  • push and pull toys
  • pounding and shaping toys
  • books

Three- to five-year-olds like to experiment with imaginary situations and have toys that are close companions:

  • nontoxic art supplies
  • pretend toys (play money, telephones)
  • teddy bears or dolls
  • outdoor toys such as a tricycle with a helmet

Five- to nine-year-olds like to be challenged with complex games that teach specific skills and concepts:

  • arts and crafts kits
  • puppets
  • jump ropes
  • miniature dolls and action figures

Nine- to fourteen-year-olds develop life-long skills, hobbies and enjoy team sports:

  • hand-held electronic games
  • board games
  • sports equipment
  • model kits
  • musical instruments
